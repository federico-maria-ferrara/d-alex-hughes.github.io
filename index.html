I am a Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Political Science and International Relations at the University of Geneva. Before starting my doctoral studies, I obtained a Master of Science in International Political Economy from the London School of Economics and Political Science and a Bachelor's Degree in Economics and Social Sciences from Bocconi University.

My research interests span several topics in international political economy. My Ph.D. thesis project explores the politics of unconventional monetary policy in Europe during the Sovereign Debt Crisis. In other parallel projects, I investigate the political determinants and consequences of international trade imbalances in advanced industrial economies. My empirical work is mostly based on automated text analysis tools and time-series cross-section methods.

Please see my projects page for more information about my research.

In September 2018 I will start a Ph.D. Traineeship in the Global Media Relations Division of the DG-Communications at the European Central Bank.
